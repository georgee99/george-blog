The Hamilton Khaki Field Automatic 42mm is one of those watches that gets recommended everywhere as a first real watch. 
After owning only quartz watches, I thought this was a step in the right direction. I ended up going with the 42mm version, 
and this is what it's been like owning and wearing it.

---

### First Impressions

The first thing I noticed when I saw it in person was how clean and simple it looked. There was something special about seeing my reflection
in the dial as I tried it on. The photos online didn't really do it justice.

On the wrist, the 42mm definitely has some presence. I was a big fan of the larger size, although Reddit had some different 
[opinions](https://www.reddit.com/r/HamiltonWatches/comments/1nmkh1t/my_first_hamilton/).

I was a big fan of the brown strap. For me, it gives the watch a military and field appearance.

---

### Design & Build

The design is very simple and straightforward. Black dial, clear numerals, and no unnecessary details. It's easy to read at a single glance, 
which is exactly what you'd want from a field watch.

The case has a brushed finish which I much prefer over something flashy. The sapphire crystal is a nice touch too. Overall, 
it feels solid without being heavy.

---

### Daily Wear Experience

It's comfortable enough to wear all day, and it works with pretty much anything. I've worn it with casual outfits, work, 
and even slightly dressy.

In terms of accuracy, it's been good enough for daily use. I'm not tracking it precisely, but I haven't noticed anything that 
would bother me.

---

### Verdict

Overall, I can see why this watch is recommended so often.

It's simple, reliable, and easy to wear every day. It might not stand out or grab attention, but that's kind of the point.

If you're looking for your first automatic watch or just something versatile you can wear all the time, this is a really solid choice.
